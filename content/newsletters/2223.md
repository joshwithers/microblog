<p><strong>Inherent problems in the internet of 2022</strong></p> 
<p>Some inherent problems in the internet of 2022, in my humble opinion:</p> <ol> <li>Everyone is too exposed to everyone else, for example, it's wild that anyone and everyone can read these words I'm typing. It's beautiful and wild, but ultimately we aren't born ready to be so exposed. There's the smallest number of celebrities that have successfully been in the public spotlight for their whole lives and come out unharmed, and even those that keep a positive public identity have conspiracy theories made up about them (Hi, Tom Hanks). I don't think we were made to be in community with the whole planet.</li> <li>Many people want to be more highly exposed, and believe that they are not exposed enough, and think everyone else needs to be exposed to them, their thoughts, their art, their work. We don't. I'm actually ok with not knowing what Kanye West thinks at the moment.</li> <li>What happens if the extremists don't live in the light but decamp off to the shadows? Do they become a problem for society? Do they impose on your and my own safety?</li> </ol>

<p><strong>Travel money tips: Up Bank vs. Wise vs. Qantas Business Money (Airwallex)</strong></p> 
<p>We're in Mexico at the moment and I have thoughts on spending Australian money abroad. Four years ago today I became an <em>Up Bank</em> customer, and it's one of the best things I've done.</p> 
<p><img src="https://joshwithers.blog/uploads/2022/928af5c9fa.png" width="600" height="532" alt="" style="max-width: 100%; height: auto;"></p> 
<p>The original sell was that if you had an <em>Up debit Mastercard</em> you could turn up at an international ATM or EFTPOS machine and swipe away without getting dodgy international fees. The payment would be made at the current rate, nothing dodgy or stupid. They lived up to that promise, and they still do today. Britt and I have moved all of our personal banking to Up and love everything about the bank, the app, the debit Mastercards, everything.</p> 
<p>If you're travelling overseas soon I could not recommend <em>Up</em> more. I started as an <em>Up</em> customer for international travel only, and after using the app more and more we became full-time customers.</p> 
<p>The only thing <em>Up</em> couldn't do inside itself was to purchase an international currency and spend that currency overseas. You might want to do this if you are travelling somewhere for a longer period of time than the week or two a holiday might be, like us moving to Mexico. (Side note: Up does have a current partnership with Wise, and I understand it's just an easier way to transfer money to Wise.)</p> 
<p>It looked like the Australian dollar would be going down on the Mexican peso over the next few months so I wanted to hedge against this and purchase some pesos. I have already used <em>Wise</em> (formerly Transferwise) for many international payments for our business, but hadn't used the issued debit cards or used the international currency bank account feature yet.</p> 
<p>Qantas had also launched a "business-grade" version of its <em>Qantas Travel Money</em> product called <em>Qantas Business Money</em> and they had big Qantas points offer available recently, so I took the opportunity to use both products. I moved a considerable amount of Australian Dollars to <em>Qantas Business Money</em> to help us purchase a car here, and then moved a similar amount to <em>Wise</em> for spending money. <em>Qantas Business Money</em> is a Qantas service provided by <em>Airwallex</em>, a Melbourne company with a handful of offices worldwide.</p> 
<p><img src="https://joshwithers.blog/uploads/2022/9cab3948da.jpg" width="600" height="800" alt="" style="max-width: 100%; height: auto;"></p> 
<p>A few points on the three different travel money products.</p> <ul> <li> <em>Up</em> has been seamless and worked (AUD-&gt;MXN) all the time at point of sale and ATM.</li> <li>My Australian-issued <em>American Express</em> has not worked (AUD-&gt;MXN) at point of sometimes.</li> <li>The <em>Wise</em> card has worked (MXN-&gt;MXN) at point of sale all but once, and I'm betting that was something at the gas station, as the card worked inside the gas station but not at the attendant's EFTPOS machine on the forecourt.</li> <li>I transferred Mexican pesos from <em>Wise</em> to a Mexican bank account on a Sunday evening Mexico time and by the time I'd woken up I had received confirmation it had worked and the recipient had received the funds.</li> <li>I transferred Mexican pesos from <em>Qantas Business Money</em> to buy a car on Friday morning at 11am Los Cabos time and it's just gone 1pm Monday morning and the money still hasn't arrived to the recipient's account.</li> <li>AirWallex support is operational 9-5 Monday to Friday Melbourne time. Even inside that time the phone number isn't answered, and neither is the San Francisco office's phone number answered. At this stage I am assuming the Airwallex and Qantas Business Money support team is a concrete block in the corner of the office.</li> <li>After doing business in a different country for a week <strong>I could not recommend against <em>Qantas</em> and <em>Airwallex</em> more. Do not use them if you want transfers to happen swiftly and wish to have support when things go wrong.</strong> I've had previous issues with the <em>Qantas Travel Money</em> built into the <em>Qantas Frequent Flyer</em> card, I don't think anyone at Qantas has actually ever used these products abroad, so neither should you.</li> <li>After three days silence a Qantas Business Money staff member told me that they needed to check for fraud and safety which would be fine if this was communicated, but also they promised the transaction would take place as banks opened today and it has not.</li> </ul> 
<p>Which banking products should you use?
- If you are happy with making withdrawals and payments at the going rate at the time, I recommend using Up.
- If you want to buy foreign currency and spend it abroad, use Wise.
- I've also had success swiping my <em>ANZ Frequent Flyer Blank</em> Visa card but I have only carried that as a backup.
- I haven't tested all the Australian bank credit cards, I understand many of them are fine.</p>
