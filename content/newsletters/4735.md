<p>As a family we travel with a MacBook Pro, iPad mini, two iPhones, Apple Watch, and a series of things that require either USB-A, USB-C, or micro USB to charge. We‚Äôve chosen gadgets and utilities that are all USB-C, with exceptions where necessary.</p> 
<p>My DJI Magic 3 drone batteries need 65W but my current travel charger‚Äôs 65W apparently doesn‚Äôt quite meet the needs the DJI three-battery USB-C charger, so the 96W Apple original MBP charger does the MacBook and the drone batteries.</p> 
<p>For a family of four travelling globally for a year it was hard to figure out the exact right kit, but we got there in the end.</p> 
<p>So my worldwide travel solution has been
- two Anker 543 Chargers (65W II), these have two USB-A ports, a USB-C 45W port and a USB-C 20W port
- my original 96W MacBook charger.</p> 
<p>Each of these, including the MacBook charger, takes as an input the figure 8 lead. <a href="https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/IEC_60320">Figure 8 leads, or IEC C7</a> leads as they‚Äôre technically known, are very easy to procure locally, most Airbnb‚Äôs even have multiple devices already using them, they‚Äôre inexpensive, and they‚Äôre small to carry.</p> 
<p><img src="https://joshwithers.blog/uploads/2023/61ff7178fol.-ac-uf8941000-ql80-.jpeg" width="600" height="356" alt="" style="max-width: 100%; height: auto;"></p> 
<p>So I buy three IEC C7 leads for each region we‚Äôre travelling to. If we weren‚Äôt on a year long trip I‚Äôd hold onto the leads but suitcase space is at a premium. So far I‚Äôve needed four different leads, a set for Australia, Mexico/USA, then the European kind, and finally the best of all - the British variety. The Brits don‚Äôt get much right but they figured out electrical points and plugs really well.</p> 
<p>I also have this assortment of cables
- 4x Thunderbolt 4/USB-C cables about 25cm long. These suit most charging, data, plugging-in situations.
- USB-C lightning leads for the iPhones 
- the Apple MagSafe travel dock for Watch and iPhone
- and the get out of jail free card is my USB-A to Lightning + USB-C + Micro-USB three-headed plug.</p>
